🌿 BIOLOGY: ECOSYSTEM (Class Notes)
1. Meaning of Ecosystem

An ecosystem is a functional unit of nature where living organisms (plants, animals, microbes) interact with each other and with the non-living environment (air, water, soil).

👉 Example: pond, forest, desert, aquarium

2. Components of Ecosystem
A. Biotic (Living)
Producers: plants, algae (make food by photosynthesis)
Consumers:
Primary: herbivores (deer, rabbit)
Secondary: carnivores (frog, small fish)
Tertiary: top carnivores (lion, shark)
Decomposers: bacteria, fungi (break down dead matter)
B. Abiotic (Non-living)
sunlight
temperature
water
soil
minerals
air
3. Food Chain

Sequence showing who eats whom.

Example:
Grass → Grasshopper → Frog → Snake → Eagle

👉 Energy flows in one direction only

4. Food Web
Interconnected food chains
More realistic than food chain
Provides stability to ecosystem
5. Ecological Pyramid

Shows energy, biomass, or number at each trophic level.

Pyramid of Energy → always upright
Pyramid of Numbers → may vary
Pyramid of Biomass → depends on ecosystem
6. Energy Flow
Starts from Sun
Plants capture energy
Only ~10% energy transfers to next level (10% law)
7. Decomposition

Steps:

Fragmentation
Leaching
Catabolism
Humification
Mineralization
8. Importance of Ecosystem
Maintains balance in nature
Supports life cycles
Recycling of nutrients
Provides resources
